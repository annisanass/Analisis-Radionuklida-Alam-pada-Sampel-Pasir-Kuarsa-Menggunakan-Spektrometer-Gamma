
# Project Summary

This project presents an analysis of natural radionuclide concentrations in quartz sand samples collected from former tin mining areas in Bangka Belitung, Indonesia, using gamma spectrometry. The study focuses on identifying radionuclides from the uranium and thorium decay series, including Pb-214, Bi-214, K-40, Pb-212, Ac-228, and Tl-208.

Gamma-ray spectra were processed using Y-Spect software with an IAEA nuclide library to obtain activity concentrations in Bq/kg. Supporting datasets include raw measurement outputs such as net count rate, measurement error, and minimum detectable activity (MDA), as well as processed final concentration data for each sample.

The results indicate relatively high concentrations of Ac-228 and Tl-208, suggesting significant gamma radiation levels in the study area. These findings highlight potential environmental and occupational health risks, particularly for individuals exposed to the mining site over long periods.

Overall, this repository provides complete supporting data from raw measurements to final processed results, ensuring transparency and reproducibility of the analysis.
